\# Party Supply Comparison: Lakehouse vs. Bro House



\## Key Takeaways

\* \*\*Shared Essential Items:\*\* Both spreadsheets prioritize a similar set of "party essentials," including disposables (garbage bags, plastic cups, plastic utensils), food staples (burgers, buns, chili), and entertainment (wireless/Bluetooth speakers).

\* \*\*Informal Quantity Pattern:\*\* A distinct pattern exists where quantities are described using informal, non-numeric terms like "nuff," "enough," "alot," or "big ole box." This suggests a "bring enough for the group" mentality rather than precise inventory management.

\* \*\*Bulk Sourcing:\*\* Both lists reference "Costco" or "big ole box," indicating that items are typically purchased in bulk to accommodate a large group.

\## Quantity Patterns

The most striking similarity is the use of the term \*\*"nuff"\*\* (or variations like "enuff" and "enough") to denote quantity:



\* \*\*Bro House Supplies:\*\* Uses "nuff," "enuff," or "enough" for 10 different items (e.g., Garbage bags, Burgers, Hot dogs, Buns).

\* \*\*Lakehouse! Supplies:\*\* Uses "nuff," "enough," or "Enough" for 5 different items (e.g., Chicken, Ping Pong Balls, buns, chilli stuff).
